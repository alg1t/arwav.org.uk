Arts, Al And Allotments


Despite having a lifelong passion for painting, I have always taken an interest in to the psychology of why these needs exist. I'm aware that my own motivation is largely derived from concerns with the limitations of personal freedom, specifically within the context of poorly built environments, not just in terms of "tinbath poverty", but with a "green poverty", an aesthetic deficit, that has largely brought about by the disestablishment of man from nature.

A typical place, that many of us were exposed to, is represented by the preceeding image which was taken at the time I was resident. Our home was situated directly below the coal-fired power station, in what would be regarded as a typical Lancastrian town. This was where my formative experiences of the world developed, i.e confined within a provincial pocket of manufacturing, my bedroom window effectively framing the backdrop to the imagery of heavy industry, producing both goods and identities. I would not consider it wholly unreasonable to regard this some-what Dantesque scene, a landscape of Marcusian Man amongst the smoking stacks, as an additional candidate for Betjeman's ire. These areas, consisting of people, who through historical circumstance, dispossessed via the common enclosures, were thrown to the machine for perpetuity, from the spinning Jenny to the modern microcontroller and microcontrolling of Taylorism, a menchanic let loose to demean us all. For the majority, our entire lived experience has been captured within its insidious reach.

Their exists a certain irony within the focal point, in that the station was owned by a company called Manweb and despite its utility, serious scrutiny could percieve this scene of mass industrialised terraforming as a Blakeian aberration whose effluence and visual imposition was conferred not just to the lungs, but also the senses of the poor. For those caught, it was an environmental assault - the broke got the smoke. Was the 'civil society' ever really delivered? With regards to the concept of human freedom, little has changed, the viscous lattice is merely evolving to manWeb 3.0. Advanced societies are essentially polymorphic, the tooling proliferates in various forms, complexity and scale, but the core essence has remained constant, to deliver it's neo-trinity of profit, efficiency and control, to the detriment of the human spirit

I was still young when an introduction to a different kind of landscape, had the unfortunate effect of instilling a sense of pastoral idealism. It came with an awareness that socio-economics largely predetermine an individuals environmental circumstance , but to leave behind the brick walls and class ceilings, I picked up a paintbrush and got an allotment.
